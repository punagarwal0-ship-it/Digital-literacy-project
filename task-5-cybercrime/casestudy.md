# Case Study: UPI "Collect Request" Fraud

## Description of the Crime
UPI/Online Payment Fraud is a financial cybercrime where attackers manipulate digital interfaces to trick users into authorizing unauthorized outgoing transfers. This scenario focuses on "Request Money" fraud, which exploits a lack of understanding regarding UPI functionality.

## Step-by-Step Scenario
1. **The Hook:** A student lists a used item (like a textbook or calculator) for sale on a campus marketplace.
2. **The Deception:** A scammer posing as a buyer contacts the student, claiming they want to pay immediately via UPI to "secure" the item.
3. **The "Payment" Link:** Instead of sending money, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" notification to the student’s UPI app.
4. **The Manipulation:** The scammer calls the student, saying: "I have sent the money; you just need to click the link and enter your UPI PIN to 'verify' that you received it".
5. **The Financial Loss:** Because a PIN is strictly for sending money, the student’s account is debited the moment the PIN is entered.

## Target and Consequences
First-year college students are frequently targeted as they are active users of digital payment tools but may not yet be familiar with "Digital Hygiene" or specific financial fraud tactics. The consequences include immediate financial loss and potential identity theft if further details are shared during the interaction.
